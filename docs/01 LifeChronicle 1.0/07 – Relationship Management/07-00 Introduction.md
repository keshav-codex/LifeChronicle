07-00 Introduction
1. Introduction

The Relationship Management Module enables users to build and manage trusted personal connections within LifeChronicle Version 1.0.

Relationships provide the foundation for privacy-aware profile viewing, collaborative events, shared memories, and future family-oriented features while ensuring every user retains complete ownership of their own Timeline.

The module follows a relationship invitation model, allowing users to connect only through invitation acceptance rather than public user searching.

2. Purpose

The Relationship Management module shall:

Establish trusted relationships between users.
Support relationship invitations.
Enable privacy-controlled profile viewing.
Support collaborative events.
Manage relationship lifecycle.
Maintain relationship-specific nicknames.
Support configurable relationship types through Master Data.
3. Scope

The module includes:

My Relationships
Add Relationship
Relationship Invitations
Relative Profile View
Relationship Status Management
Relationship Privacy
Relationship Validation
Relationship Business Rules
4. Design Principles

The module follows these principles:

Invitation-based relationships only.
No global user search.
User-controlled privacy.
Configuration-driven relationship types.
Configuration-driven invitation rules.
Independent Timeline ownership.
Future-ready architecture.
5. Integration

The Relationship Management module integrates with:

Accounts
Birth Profile
Timeline
Collaborative Events
Privacy Management
Notifications
Artificial Intelligence
Master Data
6. Summary

The Relationship Management module provides a secure, privacy-aware, and configuration-driven framework for connecting users within LifeChronicle. It enables trusted relationships, supports collaboration, and serves as the foundation for controlled information sharing while preserving independent ownership of every user's Timeline.
